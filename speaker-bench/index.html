Setup notes

Cheat sheet

Sealed or ported? — EBP = Fs / Qes

  • EBP < 50 — driver wants a sealed box. Tighter bass, gentler 12 dB/oct rolloff, more forgiving of box size, better transient/group-delay behaviour.
  • EBP 50–100 — either works. Choose by goal: sealed for punch & simplicity, ported for extension & efficiency.
  • EBP > 100 — driver wants a ported box. More low-end output for the size, steeper 24 dB/oct rolloff, but rings more and unloads below tuning.

Sealed box Qtc — the "sound" knob

  • 0.5 — critically damped. Tightest transients, no overshoot, but needs the biggest box and rolls off earliest.
  • 0.707 — Butterworth, maximally flat. The safe default; F3 = Fc.
  • 0.8–1.0 — smaller box, a slight bass bump, punchier but with some overshoot. Above ~1.2 it gets boomy.

Crossover alignments (2nd order)

  • Butterworth (Q 0.71) — drivers cross at −3 dB; on-axis sum is +3 dB. Tweeter usually wired reverse polarity.
  • Linkwitz-Riley (Q 0.5) — cross at −6 dB and sums flat on-axis. In 2nd-order LR the tweeter must be wired reverse polarity to sum flat — it is 4th-order LR that sums flat in phase. The go-to even-order alignment.
  • Bessel (Q 0.58) — best transient/phase behaviour, gentler than Butterworth in the summing region.

Formulas used here

1st order: L = R/2πf · C = 1/2πfR

2nd order LP: L = R/(2πf·Q) · C = Q/(2πf·R)  — HP swaps L↔C by duality.

Sealed: α = (Qtc/Qts)²−1 · Vb = Vas/α · Fc = (Qtc/Qts)·Fs

Ported (empirical maximally-flat): Vb = 20·Qts³·³·Vas · Fb = (Vas/Vb)⁰·³¹·Fs · F3 = (Vas/Vb)⁰·⁴⁴·Fs

Port length: Lv = 23562·d²/(Fb²·Vb) − 0.73·d (cm, litres)

Zobel: Rz ≈ 1.25·Re · Cz = Le/Rz²  ·  L-pad: Rs = Z(1−10^(−dB/20)) · Rp = Z/(10^(dB/20)−1)

These are engineering approximations into ideal loads. They get you a buildable starting point; real driver curves need measurement-based design.
